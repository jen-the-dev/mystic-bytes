## Hook & thesis
In *The Crooked Banister*, Carolyn Keene crafts another thrilling mystery that showcases Nancy Drew's cleverness and determination. **Verdict:** This installment stands out as a masterclass in suspense, engaging readers with its clever plot twists and relatable themes of bravery and friendship.

## The contextual pivot
As the 48th entry in the beloved Nancy Drew Mystery Stories series, this book fits snugly within the middle-grade mystery genre. Keene's work often features young female protagonists tackling challenging situations, and here, Nancy's adventures resonate with both younger and older readers. By bridging the niche of juvenile thrillers with universal themes of courage and resourcefulness, this title remains accessible and engaging for a wider audience.

## Deep-dive critique
Keene excels in creating tension through her pacing and the structure of the narrative. The point of view remains consistently with Nancy, allowing readers to experience her thought processes and dilemmas directly, which effectively pulls us deeper into the mystery. The dialogue is snappy and authentic, reflecting the age of the characters while also contributing to the overall atmosphere of suspense. However, some scenes could benefit from more vivid descriptions; at times, the world-building feels a bit flat, which detracts from the otherwise immersive experience. The resolution is satisfying, with all threads tied up neatly, ensuring that the reader feels the reward of following Nancy's journey closely.

## Discussion launchpad
*The Crooked Banister* presents polarizing elements typical of mystery stories, such as the balance between predictability and surprise. How do readers feel about the familiar tropes of the series versus the desire for fresh, innovative plots?

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does Nancy Drew's character continue to inspire modern readers, or is she seen as outdated?  
- What role does friendship play in solving mysteries, and can it be a weakness as well as a strength?  
- How do you feel about the resolution of mysteries? Should they be neatly tied up, or left open for interpretation?  
- Can a mystery be too predictable, or is it the journey that matters?  
- How do the themes of bravery in this book compare to those in other middle-grade mysteries you've read?
