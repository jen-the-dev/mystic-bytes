## Hook & thesis
In *The Sign of the Twisted Candles*, Carolyn Keene crafts an engaging mystery that captivates young readers with its clever plot and relatable heroine. **Verdict:** This book is a delightful exploration of friendship and bravery, showcasing Nancy Drew's unwavering determination to uncover the truth.

## The contextual pivot
As the ninth installment in the beloved Nancy Drew Mystery Stories, this novel holds a special place in the children's mystery genre. It bridges the gap between young readers and the classic whodunit trope, maintaining a light-hearted yet suspenseful tone. Keene's work often centers around strong female protagonists, and Nancy serves as a role model, encouraging readers to think critically and solve problems.

## Deep-dive critique
Keene's skillful use of pacing is evident as the story unfolds with a mix of intrigue and action. The narrative is structured around Nancy's relentless pursuit of the mystery behind the twisted candles, with each chapter revealing crucial clues that maintain reader engagement. The dialogue is sharp and reflective of the characters' personalities, particularly Nancy's witty exchanges with her friends, which add depth to her character. The world-building is engaging, offering vivid descriptions of the settings that transport readers into Nancy's adventurous world. However, at times, the resolution feels a bit rushed, leaving some threads slightly unexplored, which may leave readers wanting more closure.

## Discussion launchpad
This book presents several engaging topics for discussion:
- The balance between mystery and character development: does Nancy's character overshadow the mystery?
- How does Keene's portrayal of friendship add to the story's overall theme?
- What elements of the book resonate well with contemporary young readers compared to other mystery novels?

**Questions for the room:**
