## Hook & thesis
In Keri Lake's *Anathema*, the intricate dance between horror and romance unfolds in a haunting forest where love and darkness intertwine. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully captures the essence of desire and dread, creating an intoxicating atmosphere that lingers long after the final page.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned firmly within the gothic-horror-romance genre, *Anathema* carves out its space amidst a rich tapestry of influences ranging from the Southern Gothic to the modern supernatural narrative. Lake crafts a world that resonates with echoes of authors like Anne Rice and Sarah Waters, yet distinctly channels a contemporary voice that merges traditional tropes with visceral themes. The invisible audience likely comprises fans of dark romance seeking something that transcends mere attraction, bridging niche elements with broader emotional landscapes that appeal to those drawn to the shadows of desire.

## Deep-dive critique
Lake's narrative structure deftly balances multiple points of view, enhancing the reader's connection to the characters while simultaneously amplifying the tension inherent in their relationships. The pacing, particularly in the latter half, builds a palpable sense of urgency that mirrors the characters' escalating stakes—fueled by their interactions within the Eating Woods. The lush prose envelops the reader, with descriptions that evoke both the beauty and the grotesque, reflecting the duality of the central conflict: the protagonist's struggle against the alluring yet dangerous forces of their environment. 

Character development is particularly noteworthy; the protagonists are flawed yet compelling, their desires intricately woven into the fabric of the story. Dialogue feels authentic, capturing the rawness of their emotions while propelling the plot forward. However, the thematic exploration of violence and consent may polarize readers, as it flirts with dark themes that could alienate those seeking lighter fare. In terms of resolution, while the ending offers closure, it also leaves tantalizing threads unresolved, inviting contemplation on the cyclical nature of desire and despair.

## Discussion launchpad
*Anathema* invites a rich discussion on the balance between horror and romance, and how one informs the other. The interplay of beauty and monstrosity may split readers on their reception of the work. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Lake’s approach to consent shape your understanding of the characters' motivations?  
- Do you see the Eating Woods as a character in its own right, and what does that mean for the story’s outcome?  
- In what ways does the juxtaposition of desire and violence challenge the traditional tropes of romance?  
- How effective is the dual POV in enhancing the themes of the novel?  
- Can you separate the appeal of the gothic aesthetic from the darker themes present in the narrative?
