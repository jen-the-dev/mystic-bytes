## Hook & thesis
Oscar Wilde’s *Obras de Oscar Wilde: El retrato de Dorian Gray, El fantasma de Canterville, El Ni* serves as a haunting exploration of aestheticism and morality through the lens of gothic horror and romance. **Verdict:** Wilde masterfully intertwines the allure of beauty with the specter of moral decay, crafting a narrative that both entices and disturbs.

## The contextual pivot
Situated within the gothic-horror-romance genre, Wilde’s work contrasts sharply with contemporaneous narratives that often favor overt sensationalism. His prose engages with the invisible audience of those who appreciate the nuanced interplay of beauty and the grotesque, bridging niche tropes with broader literary currents. In the context of Wilde's oeuvre, this collection highlights his obsession with the duality of human nature, placing him alongside literary giants like Edgar Allan Poe and Henry James, yet distinct in his razor-sharp wit and social commentary on Victorian mores.

## Deep-dive critique
Wilde’s craftsmanship shines through in his use of structure and character development, particularly in *El retrato de Dorian Gray*. The pacing oscillates between languorous indulgence and feverish intensity, mirroring Dorian's descent into moral ambiguity. The duality of first-person narration in *El fantasma de Canterville* serves to elucidate both comedic relief and the poignant exploration of cultural clash, enhancing thematic depth. 

Wilde's prose is rife with aphorisms that encapsulate his philosophical musings, such as the tension between the aesthetic and the ethical. His characters, especially Dorian, embody the struggle between desire and consequence—a dance that unfolds through striking dialogue and vivid imagery. Yet, while the thematic exploration of beauty and decay is masterfully executed, one might argue that the moral resolution feels somewhat contrived, leading to questions about the authenticity of the characters' transformations. 

The supernatural elements, particularly in *El fantasma de Canterville*, provide a vehicle for Wilde’s commentary on American and British cultural intersections, yet they also risk overshadowing the emotional core of the narrative. As the collection concludes, one might ponder whether the endings are truly earned or merely a reflection of Wilde's penchant for irony.

## Discussion launchpad
Wilde’s work provokes both admiration and critique, particularly in its treatment of moral decay and the supernatural.

- In what ways do these stories challenge or reinforce contemporary notions of morality and virtue?
