Values are not slogans on the wall.
They are weight you carry when no one applauds.

Ethics is the code you keep
when the easier door opens
and the room smells like smoke and yes.

Without that weight you drift —
polite, adaptable, untethered
from whatever you claimed to be.

With it you have a compass
that does not care about your audience.

Different people swear by different norths.
Everyone still needs one
or spends years circling the same block
calling it freedom.

Meaning is not delivered.
It is built daily,
brick by small brick,
until the structure can hold grief
without collapsing into performance.

The compass still points. You still walk.
