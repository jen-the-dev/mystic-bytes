Celebration is too loud a word.
Creation is quieter —
rhythm kept because the body needed it,
rhyme arriving when the grief
wanted a shape to hold.

Muses are not hired here.
Attention is.

Joy and sorrow share the stanza
without resolving the argument.
That is the craft:
not perfect display,
accurate pressure on the line.

The voice does not promise wings.
It promises return visits.

The line break holds.
