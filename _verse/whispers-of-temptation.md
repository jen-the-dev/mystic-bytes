Temptation whispers in New Orleans
where alley and jazz conspire —
pleasure priced as danger,
danger sold as romance.

The fine line is not a dance.
It is a ledger:
what you want,
what it invoices,
who pays if the room finds out.

Resistance is not virtue theater.
It is choosing the appetite
you can survive wearing.

Some whispers become addresses.
Some become warnings you mail to yourself.

The alley still holds the echo.
