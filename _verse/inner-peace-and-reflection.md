Meditation is not a vacation from consequence.
It is practice —
rumination slowed until something true
can surface without applause.

Stillness is not emptiness.
It is room to ponder
what the fast day edited out.

The world fades when you stop feeding it.
What remains is not always gentle.
Sometimes it is ledger.

Peace fills like a cup only if you hold it.
Inner light is not divine delivery.
It is attention staying put
long enough to warm the hands.

Breath finds center
the way a house finds studs —
by touch, by repeat, by error.

Thoughts lose their manager voice.
Stillness stays,
not as reward,
as infrastructure.

Reflect on your design without begging for a halo.
The path is not guaranteed divine.
It is walked.

The cup still steadies. You still breathe into it.
