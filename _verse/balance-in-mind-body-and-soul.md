Health is not the absence of collapse.
It is fortitude under ordinary weather —
form that can carry grief
without filing for bankruptcy.

Vigor is not performance for the mirror.
It is potency you feel
when stairs, sleep, and appetite
stop negotiating.

Mind, body, soul are not three offices.
They are one building
with bad intercom wiring.
When one room floods, the others smell it.

Exercise, food, rest are not virtue signals.
They are maintenance —
unsexy, repeated, the kind of love
that does not photograph well.

Wholeness is harmony with limits,
not domination of limits.

Joy is allowed here.
So is fatigue.
Balance is adjustment, not arrival.

The body still signals. You still listen.
