Compassion in New Orleans
is not performance.
It is the hand when the levee of pride breaks —
bayou whisper,
neighbor with ice,
stranger who knows the route
when the lights fail.

Shelter is a verb here.
Kind words are load-bearing.

The Crescent City teaches:
love expands by logistics,
not slogans.

Ablaze is too theatrical.
Accurate is showing up
with the tool
and staying through the ugly hour.

The ice still melts in the shared cooler.
