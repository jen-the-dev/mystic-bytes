Anger arrives like weather with teeth —
ire first, then the storm
that rearranges the furniture of reason.

Wrath is not courage.
It is heat seeking fuel:
old hurt, fresh slight,
the story you keep ready.

Indignation can be accurate.
It can also be habit
wearing righteousness
like a borrowed coat.

Enmity spreads the way fire does —
room to room,
name to name,
until everything tastes like smoke.

Vexation is the smaller cousin:
sharp, daily, exhausting,
still capable of carving channels
you will walk later without remembering why.

Bitterness is anger left to cure
until it hardens into worldview.

Name the blaze.
Do not move your furniture into it.

The ring still burns. You still choose what to feed it.
