Violence is not a metaphor here.
It is impact —
scars that keep office hours,
innocence revised without consent.

The aftermath is not a dream
one wakes from clean.
It is a body learning
which rooms are safe
and which rooms only claim to be.

Love is offered as remedy
in cheaper narratives.
This account records love
as one possible tool —
not a magic that undoes the bruise.

Shadows do not convert to light on schedule.
They lengthen.
They teach the eyes
to adjust.

The bruise keeps its calendar.
