Insecurity keeps office hours.
Doubt clocks in. Hesitancy signs the ledger.

Footing disappears in ordinary rooms —
elevator, inbox, the pause before your name.

Exposure is the fear that tells the truth:
visibility has consequences.

Misgivings ring like cheap bells.
Not every ring is fire.
Some is memory rehearsing old exits.

Paralysis is a tariff on imagination.
You pay it until you learn negotiation.

These moments do not own your name.
They are weather through the body —
uncomfortable, repeated, not a biography.

Acceptance is not surrender.
It is naming the grip
without hiring it as manager.

Resilience is walking anyway,
slow, unphotogenic, still counted.

The footing returns by use. You still stand.
