Violence is weather here.
Love and art are how New Orleans
builds shelter without pretending
the storm cancelled.

Murals do not erase harm.
They file dissent in color —
French Quarter cobbles,
Congo Square memory,
Treme shoulders bearing brass.

Each brushstroke is logistics:
care made visible,
consent in the dance,
second line as counterargument.

Eternal is declined.
Accurate is the note still sounding
when the block goes quiet.

The brass keeps answering the chaos.
