Tragedy arrives without a thesis.
It rearranges furniture.
It leaves a chair facing the wrong wall.

Triumph is a word people bring
when they cannot sit with the wreckage.
The text declines the invitation.

What remains after catastrophe
is not a climb.
It is inventory —
what still works,
what will not answer its name,
what love refuses to leave on the curb.

A scar is not a medal.
It is a map of where the weather hit.

The chair still faces the wrong wall.
