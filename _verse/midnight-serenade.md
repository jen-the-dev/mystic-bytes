Midnight in New Orleans
turns the sky into venue.
Stars emerge one note at a time —
cosmic parade,
moon as horn,
velvet as curtain.

Wonder is not innocence.
It is attendance.
You look up
because the street
already taught you
to listen for surprise.

Celestial shrine is declined.
Accurate is diamonds
you cannot own
briefly borrowed
while the city hums below.

The parade continues after you lower your eyes.
