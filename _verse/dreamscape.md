Dreams in New Orleans
do not stay indoors.
They spill onto porches,
attach to parade floats,
borrow brass
until fantasy learns sweat.

The canvas is not blank.
It is already loud.
Your vision must negotiate
with gumbo steam
and somebody's uncle's opinion.

Soar is a brochure word.
Accurate is climb —
one iron balcony at a time,
color as argument,
legacy as something heavier
than Instagram.

The float passes; the dream keeps rolling.
