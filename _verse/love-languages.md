Praise as lever.
Service as ledger.
Gifts with receipts attached.

Touch withheld until you comply —
then called romance
when you flinch.

Quality time that monitors your phone.
Space used as punishment,
returned as favor.

The healthy version is boring on paper:
consent, reciprocity, attention without surveillance.

The toxic version wears the same clothes
and knows your sizes.

You learned the difference
when kindness arrived with a price list
and called itself devotion.

The receipt is still in your pocket.
