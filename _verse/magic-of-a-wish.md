The first wish is for show —
birthday candle, polite hunger,
something the room can clap for.

The second wish is the real one:
stay, leave, punish, be chosen,
be left alone with your winnings.

Magic is the tax on wanting aloud.
Hecate counts the ones
you will not admit to the cake.

Power is not the granting.
It is discovering
what you are willing to trade
when no one is performing innocence.

The candle smoke writes the second wish.
