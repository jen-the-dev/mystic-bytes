The unknown arrives aghast —
risk without receipt,
rescue uncertain,
mind a whirlwind with opinions.

Peril fills the moment you dare.
Panic is not prophecy.
It is physiology asking for a pause
you may not afford.

Still you move.
Heart fast, truth faster:
fear remembered less
than the step taken through it.

Danger is not always teacher.
Sometimes it is simply cost.
Strength is naming cost
without hiring it as identity.

Bravery is not absence of terror.
It is continuation
with terror audible.

Open heart does not mean open wound.
It means staying in the journey
without requiring a halo at the end.

The unknown keeps its mask.
You still press forward.
