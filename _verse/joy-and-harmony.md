Music does not fix the race.
It pauses the invoice.

Melody enters the chest
before politics can name it.
Hands clap.
For a measure,
strangers agree on tempo.

Harmony is not permanent.
It is chorus —
many throats,
one held note,
then release.

The secret never unlocks fully.
That is the point.
You borrow the magical place
and return to the pain
with better rhythm.

The chorus invites you back in.
