Virtue is not a crown you inherit.
It is the harder receipt —
what you paid when no one graded you.

Righteousness begins as argument
with the self you prefer to market:
the honest sentence,
the mercy that costs status,
the no spoken when yes is cheaper.

Kindness without witness
still counts in the ledger
you keep at 3 a.m.

Uprightness is not posture.
It is repetition —
the same choice
when the audience leaves.

The receipt is still in your hand.
