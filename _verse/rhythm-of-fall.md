Fall in New Orleans
does not apologize for change.
Leaves jazz their colors,
harvest fills the table,
then bare branch
teaches the other invoice.

Cycle is not cruelty only.
It is rhythm —
ebb like blues,
rebirth like second line,
beauty filed beside ending.

Big Easy truth:
even surrender
can dance if the band agrees.

The brass still plays for the bare trees.
