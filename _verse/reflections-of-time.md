Eternity is unavailable for inspection.
What the body holds is minutes —
spent, unspent, misfiled.

Memory outlives the subject
without granting immortality.
It returns at odd hours,
demanding room rent in the chest.

Cherish is not a command here.
It is a practice
with no guarantee of comfort.

The dead do not need our poetry.
We need the honesty
of what they altered.

The clock strikes without commentary.
