Patience is not passivity dressed polite.
It is stoic weather —
calm that does not pretend the storm is imaginary.

Wait with composure when the world sharpens.
Humility here is letting the course
show its hand before you rename it.

Gentleness is not weakness.
It is strength without theater.

Chaos arrives in hue and noise.
The placid lake at night
is not naivete.
It is practice.

Solace is a tool you reach for
when cruelty makes speed look virtuous.

Stoic grace is not numbness.
It is choosing the longer breath
so the answer is yours,
not the adrenaline's.

Serenity is font, not finish line —
you return to it,
spill it,
return again.

The lake still holds. You still wait with your whole weight.
