Loneliness is vast acoustics —
void that sounds like verdict,
ache that chills without weather.

Routine amplifies absence.
You hear your own breath
and mistake it for proof
no one is coming.

Seclusion becomes companion
by default, not design.
The heart feels unkind
because hunger is not polite.

Overcoming is not triumph montage.
It is one reach —
message, knock, porch light left on
for a connection that might not arrive on schedule.

Fear argues for staying sealed.
Perseverance is continuing
while the silence keeps score.

Hope here is not a poster.
It is a match struck
in a drafty house you still inhabit.

Power is not volume.
It is staying readable
long enough to be found.

The chain still breaks link by link. You still reach.
