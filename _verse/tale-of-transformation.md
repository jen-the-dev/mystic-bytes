A bean dreams of liqueur —
not destiny,
just a hotter identity
with better gossip.

Roasting is the first argument with heat.
Brewing is the second.
Sugar and cream negotiate.
Hours stir the outcome.

Transformation is not inspiration.
It is process
you cannot rush
without scorching the proof.

New Orleans tastes like the labor,
not the moral.

The cup cools on the counter.
