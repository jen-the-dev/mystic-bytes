Perplexity is not stupidity.
It is sand under the footing —
core unclear, path twisted,
fear dressed as curiosity.

Confusion reigns while you decipher
a game that did not publish its rules.
Clues are scarce because you are early,
not because you are cursed.

You intervene anyway.
That is the human tax on unknowns.

Answers persist because hearts persist —
not nobly, stubbornly.

The great unknown may be sinister.
It may be blown out of proportion.
The riddle stays untamed
until you stop requiring it to be polite.

Search deep.
Secrets you keep are also keys you hoard.

Freedom is not guaranteed at the lock.
Still you turn what you have.

The riddle still holds. You still dig for the key.
