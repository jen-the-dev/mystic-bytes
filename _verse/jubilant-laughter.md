Laughter in New Orleans
is infrastructure.
French Quarter punchline,
Bourbon Street belly laugh,
brass band tease —
each says survive anyway.

Symphony is declined.
Healing is not innocence.
It is noise you choose
when the bill arrives.

Brigade is accurate:
giggle shared,
burden lightened one notch,
belonging restored
before the set ends.

The laugh still bounces off the next balcony.
