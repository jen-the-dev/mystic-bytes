Perspective is lens, not verdict.
Context bends the scene —
narrow angle, broad expanse,
same street, different cost.

Frame of mind shifts the way brass shifts key:
suddenly the ordinary depth
you walked past yesterday
has architecture.

Beauty was not absent.
It was unprioritized.

Horizon line moves when you step back.
Alignment is not happiness.
It is seeing honestly
from more than one station.

Glory is not required.
Hue is enough.

Selective is not always cowardice.
Sometimes it is mercy —
choosing what you can carry
without lying about the weight.

Shift the view.
Keep the receipts.

The lens still turns. You still choose where to stand.
