Grief in New Orleans
does not ask for privacy.
It walks the block,
sits at the table,
keeps the same accent
as laughter.

Resilience is not triumph.
It is fragment work —
heart broken,
hands still washing rice,
still answering the door.

The city's embrace is not soft.
It is humid and insistent.
Solace arrives with brass nearby,
as if joy were witness,
not verdict.

The rice water runs clear. The door still opens.
