Color in New Orleans
is not decoration.
It is testimony —
vermilion for appetite,
cobalt for the river's calm lie,
emerald for what survived the storm,
gold for the stage light
that makes exhaustion visible.

Pastel façades lie gently.
Treme tells the louder truth.

Harmony is contrast here.
The eye learns to read trouble
and celebration
in the same saturation.

The palette keeps speaking after the parade ends.
