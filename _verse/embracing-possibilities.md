Possibility is not a motivational sky.
It is inventory —
doors unopened, tools unused,
appetite still arguing with schedule.

Vision without motion is décor.
Foresight earns its name
when the leap costs something real.

Obstacles are not insults from the universe.
They are friction tests:
what you keep, what you shed,
what you admit you want.

Each step reveals a corridor
you could not see from the couch.
Not every corridor is virtue.
Some are exits you needed years ago.

Destiny is not a crown.
It is clay —
shaped by nerve, by repetition,
by the willingness to look foolish
long enough to learn.

Explore like someone who might fail in public.
That is where the interesting light enters.

The door stays unopened until you turn the handle.
