Patience in Louisiana
is not virtue signaling.
It is cypress shade,
Spanish moss,
the second line
teaching your feet
that arrival
can be slow
and still be joy.

The steamboat does not hurry for your poem.
The turtle on the bank
has politics you cannot afford.

New Orleans offers repose
with teeth underneath —
wait long enough
and the current
tells you who you are.

The moss still sways after the brass stops.
