Inspiration is a spark, not a salary.
It arrives — whimsy, insight, elation —
then expects you to work.

Revelation opens the eyes.
It does not close the ledger.

Fervor lifts you past the norm
until gravity invoices you.

Imagination keeps the hands warm.
Talent is what remains
when the spark has gone out
and you continue anyway.

This is fuel for schemes and failures alike.
Magic in creation is labor dressed as appetite.

Let it flow wild, yes —
but wild still needs a channel
or it floods the house and calls itself art.

Follow where it leads
knowing some doors are traps with excellent lighting.

The spark still catches. You still feed the fire.
