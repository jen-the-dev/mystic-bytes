The house registers quiet
against the number of cups in the sink.

A place is set.
It remains unsettled.

Sorrow, in this account,
is not a lesson plan.
It is weight —
the body learns its contour
without requesting the syllabus.

Tears function as egress,
not as evidence of progress.

On certain evenings
a figure pauses at a doorway
whose air still holds a prior occupant,
and does not cross the threshold.
The text calls this love
but does not resolve it.

The narrative declines
to knit the fracture
into something admirable.
The fracture persists.
It answers to a name.

The extra cup stays on the table.
