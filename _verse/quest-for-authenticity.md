The world runs on masks and small deceits.
Candor reads obsolete
until someone pays its price in public.

Truth is not a mood.
It is bond —
reality kept in view
when performance would be cheaper.

Facts are pillars, yes,
also weapons,
also shelter,
depending on who holds them.

Genuine honesty unlocks doors
pretense only paints.
Pretend long enough
and you forget which face is load-bearing.

Convictions must guide action,
not caption it.
Clarity is not purity.
It is alignment you can defend
after the room goes quiet.

Face the day without requiring applause.
Freedom is not lightness.
It is living readable.

The mask still slips. You still choose the true line.
