The tree is not sacred because I said so.
It is tall because time allowed,
roots busy in dark,
branches hosting creatures
who do not know your name.

Strength is shelter offered
without contract.
Beauty is green
doing its job.

Thirteen hears the promise adults make —
protect the forest —
and knows it is homework
for a lifetime,
not a poster.

The timber still whispers after you pass.
