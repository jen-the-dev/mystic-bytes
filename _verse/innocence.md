Innocence in New Orleans
is observed, not owned.
It appears on porches,
in parade sideline faces,
before the body learns
what the brass costs.

Pure is a word adults misuse.
Accurate is unguarded —
brief,
expensive,
already fading by adolescence.

The city does not promise fairness.
It promises rhythm.
Innocence learns the downbeat early.

The downbeat still plays after the child looks away.
