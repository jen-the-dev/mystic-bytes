Forgiveness is not a favor to the wound.
It is labor in the body —
the jaw unclenching
after the story has finished
its first draft of revenge.

You may release someone
and still remember the knife.
Memory is not malice.
It is inventory.

The beauty of forgiveness
is not that the harm glows —
it is that you stop paying interest
on a debt
the other person is not collecting.

Some doors stay closed.
That is also a form of mercy
toward the self.

The jaw unclenches one notch further.
