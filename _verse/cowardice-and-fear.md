Fear is not moral failure.
It is the body reading the room
and finding the exit
before the mind writes a speech.

Cowardice, in cheap stories,
is a label for people
who survived by hiding.
This account uses neither praise nor contempt.

The spineless hour is documented:
object used as shield,
challenge deferred,
dread occupying the chest
like bad furniture.

Change, if it comes,
is one step —
not heroism,
motion.

The exit sign stays lit.
