Family in New Orleans
is not a diagram.
It is thread —
matriarch memory,
patriarch stubbornness,
cousin at the door
with a pot and an opinion.

Tapestry is labor, not décor.
Gumbo steam is sacrament.
Second line feet learn belonging
before language can explain it.

Symphony is declined.
Harmony is Tuesday dinner
with discord in the gravy
and love still ladled.

The next chapter weaves itself in the steam.
