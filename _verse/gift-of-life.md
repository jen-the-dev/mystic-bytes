The gift of life is not a bow.
It is breath on loan,
guardians holding the door
while you learn
what their mistakes cost.

Mother, Father —
not idols.
Laborers.
The hug and the start
are real currency.

Forever and ever is a hymn.
Accurate is inheritance:
bone, habit, argument,
the belonging you spend adulthood
either honoring or escaping.

Nobody replaces them.
Some days you wish someone could.

The loan keeps compounding quietly.
