Meditation is not escape.
It is sitting with the animal
that lives behind your plans
until it stops performing calm.

Breath in —
not victory,
truce.

Breath out —
the rumination loosens
its collar one notch.

The world fades
only as much as you allow.
Zen is not a trophy room.
It is the moment you notice
you are not your worst headline.

The center does not hold forever.
It holds long enough
to tell the truth once.

The breath returns before you do.
