Poetry is not a wing factory.
It is the room where joy and sorrow
are forced to share a chair
without pretending to agree.

Stanzas are not rehearsals for applause.
They are receipts —
what the body noticed
when prose wanted to behave.

Rhythm is breath with opinions.
Meter is optional.
Truth is not.

The allure is not enchantment.
It is permission to leave the ending
unfixed.

The chair is still occupied.
