Pride is lion-shaped —
chest inflated, presence announced,
dignity and appetite in one hide.

Pomp follows like a parade route.
Ego fills the boast
until confidence forgets calibration
and cuts the room.

Still we want it:
pride in self, in place,
voice that carries without begging.

Noble pride is strength without theater —
roar optional, spine required.

Arrogance is pride without restraint,
deceiving you with worth
borrowed from volume.

Journey to achieve needs a mirror.
Worth should shine as earned weather,
not performed thunder.

The lion still roars. You still choose which pride to feed.
