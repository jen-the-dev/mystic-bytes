Joy arrives as power —
euphoria without application form,
contentment spreading head to toe
when trouble is briefly outbid.

Elation does not negotiate boundaries.
Cheer composes itself differently
from every other appetite.

Like a butterfly released,
sorrow gets smiled at for a moment.
Delight is amazing because it is rare,
not because it is permanent.

Joy does not discriminate on paper.
In practice it visits unevenly —
pure when present,
not a warranty against dark nights.

Road less traveled is romance.
Peace and tranquility are destinations
people sell when they have not read the map.

Harmony is not eternal here.
Joy is witness: body confirming
the room was worth entering.

The butterfly still lifts. You still notice the flight.
