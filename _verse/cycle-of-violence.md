Violence is harm without morality —
aggression that scars past healing,
injury that keeps interest.

Attack on one is assault on room.
Tumult makes bodies fall.
Pain cannot be undone;
cycle repeats until refused.

Aftermath is tragic scene, not dream.
Innocence taken; jarred depth remains.

Rise above is labor, not poster.
Love as guidance means break the link —
not romance, wrench.

Vicious chain shunned by choice repeated.
Heal is slow weather.

The chain still pulls. You still refuse the next link.
