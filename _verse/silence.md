Silence in New Orleans
is not absence.
It is the rest between brass —
moss in City Park,
pause after the refrain,
cathedral breath,
ancestor whisper
you cannot quote cleanly.

Symphony is declined.
Counter-rhythm is accurate.
The city is loud enough
to make stillness
feel like luxury
and like warning.

Inner voice arrives
when the horns stop
long enough
for you to hear the bill due.

The moss holds the hush after the refrain.
