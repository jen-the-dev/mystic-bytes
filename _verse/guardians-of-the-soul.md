Pets in New Orleans
are not accessories.
They are guardians —
dog at the second line,
cat in Garden District sun,
turtle teaching slow resilience,
parrot mocking jazz
with unfair accuracy.

Love here is mutual logistics:
bowl, walk, vet,
warmth exchanged
without contract language.

House becomes home
when another species
files a claim on your schedule.

The wag still outvotes the argument.
