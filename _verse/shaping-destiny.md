The future is potential, not permission.
Destiny calls —
you still choose whether to answer
with craft or with fantasy.

Hope unfolds when fed by labor.
Imminent possibility is not a delivery date.

Innovate is a verb with splinters.
Fate guides only as much
as you confuse momentum with mandate.

Foresee peace and unity
if you must —
then look at the invoice.
Bright tomorrow is built,
not prophesied.

Power accrues daily in small units:
what you refuse,
what you repeat,
what you finally name.

Mold the future with open hands,
not clenched doctrine.
Dreams are materials,
not managers.

Destiny is in the work,
not the poster.

The clay still waits. Your hands still shape it.
