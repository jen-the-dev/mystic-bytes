The battle is not outdoors.
It is interior —
two hungers sharing a ribcage,
one calling itself virtue,
one calling itself truth.

Hate feeds on bile.
Love feeds on proximity.
Both can destroy the room
if left unattended.

Shield metaphors are declined.
What works is naming the target
before the blade picks it for you.

Debris is shared property.

The ribcage still hosts both.
