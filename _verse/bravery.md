Fear in New Orleans
knows the map —
gas lamps, alley logic,
bayou dark,
the drum in Congo Square
that sounds like judgment
until you move.

Bravery is not absence.
It is walking anyway
while the city watches
with voodoo patience
and brass forgiveness.

The Crescent City does not hide fear.
It schedules it between sets.

You remember the walk,
not the fright —
the feet that kept time
when the heart filed protest.

The drum cadence still outvotes the fear.
