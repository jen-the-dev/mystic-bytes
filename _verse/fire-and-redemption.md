Anger in New Orleans does not whisper.
It arrives with heat and brass in the blood —
resentment keeping time
like a bad jazz break
that will not resolve.

Bitterness is not a curse from elsewhere.
It is chemistry
left on the stove too long.

Redemption is not guaranteed
by forgiveness language.
Sometimes the work is turning down the flame
before the house files for loss.

Compassion may arrive.
It is not a refund.

The stove is still warm.
