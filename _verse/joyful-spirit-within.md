A smile is not always confession.
Sometimes it is architecture —
amusement arranged on the face
while the room decides what to do with it.

Elation spreads the way heat does:
dimple, beam, the small betrayal
that pleasure was present
even when the day argued otherwise.

Joy climbs without permission.
It does not require a clean ledger.

Laughter adds sparkle the way grit adds traction —
not decoration,
evidence.

Hope assigns itself in glimpses.
You do not have to earn the glimpse first.

Glitter is the wrong metaphor.
Light is closer:
brief, local, enough to read by.

Happiness here is not a contract.
It is weather that sometimes
chooses your mouth.

The light still arrives. You still let it show.
