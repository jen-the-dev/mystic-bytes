The heart does not shatter cleanly.
It splinters —
edges that catch on sleep,
on songs,
on the name you were told not to say.

Betrayal is not a lesson plan.
It is inventory taken without consent.

Mending is not a montage.
It is gathering fragments
with cut hands
and refusing to call the blood proof of growth.

Fresh start is a phrase for calendars.
The body keeps the old diagram.

Some pieces will not fit again.
That is also data.

The splinter still catches on the third stair.
