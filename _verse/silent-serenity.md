Serenity arrives without announcement —
not absence of noise,
absence of argument
with what is.

The room holds its breath
the way a held breath holds you:
temporary, deliberate,
not a permanent visa.

Silence can be sanctuary
or the pause before the wrong yes.

Learn which you are standing in
before you call it holy.

The hush remains after the door closes.
