An instant passes like a struck match —
light, heat, then the wick
remembering it was ordinary.

Every present is more than pleasant
if you stop performing long enough
to taste it.

Hold the breath not to freeze time
but to notice time
was already leaving.

Sun on skin.
Distant hum.
Senses stealing you from the errand list
for one honest second.

Moments that linger become memory —
not because you captured them,
because you were there
when they happened.

Treasure is not nostalgia hoarding.
It is respect for the small doorways
meaning walks through.

Cherish is a verb, not a shelf.
Hold close what teaches you
how to unwind without disappearing.

The match still strikes. You still notice the flame.
