Imagination is not a vacation.
It is a workshop with the door left open —
drafts, sparks, half-built machines
that may never love you back.

Invention begins as appetite:
something seen sideways,
something the ordinary day refused to finish.

Originality is not purity.
It is appetite disciplined
until it becomes form.

The mind paints rooms no landlord approved.
Some rooms are cathedrals.
Some are traps with excellent lighting.

Limitless is a flattering lie.
Even wonder has edges —
frame, fuel, the wrists that tire.

Still the canvas demands another stroke.
Progress eats imagination
and calls the meal necessity.

Use the gift like a tool, not a crown.
Build worlds that can survive daylight.

The canvas stays wet. You still reach in.
