Rain is not divine mail.
It is water doing its job —
fine drops on leaves,
mud activated,
air washed enough
to breathe differently for an hour.

Renewal is not erasure.
Past pain does not vanish
because the sidewalk glistens.
It relocates.

Thirteen is old enough
to like the sound
without calling it salvation.

The rhythmic flow continues after you go inside.
