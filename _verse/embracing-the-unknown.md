Wonder is not childishness preserved.
It is appetite kept alive
after the world offered easier narcotics.

Awe is the body noticing scale —
beauty, strife, the thing too large
for the first vocabulary you owned.

Curiosity is the honest trespass:
what is this, what does it cost,
who am I after I know.

Perplexity is not failure.
It is the mind admitting shoreline.

The unknown is not an enemy.
It is horizon —
always receding,
always inviting one more step.

Lose wonder and life flattens to errands.
Keep wonder and thunder returns
in small rooms — inbox, kitchen, the pause
before a difficult yes.

To be in awe is to stay teachable
without begging to be saved.

The horizon still recedes. You still walk toward it.
