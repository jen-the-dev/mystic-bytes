Faith, here, is not a brochure.
It is fealty to something you cannot outsource —
a vow kept when the room goes quiet
and applause rots fast.

Trust is not naivete.
It is structure:
belief load-bearing enough
to hold you upright through the ugly week.

Conviction fuels the climb
when certainty frays at the hem.
Dogma gets a bad name
because people wear it like armor
instead of bone.

In darkness you do not need a slogan.
You need one true sentence
you can repeat without lying.

Strength is not denial.
It is staying in the weather
long enough to learn its grammar.

The light people quote at you
must be the one you tested
against your own failures.

The light still burns inward. You still tend it.
