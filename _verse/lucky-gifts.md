Luck is not moral verdict.
It is weather that favored you once
while someone else stood in the open.

Gratefulness is naming the shelter
without insisting you deserved the roof
more than the rain deserved you.

Gifts arrive uninvited —
the friend, the timing, the body
that failed a worse way.

Grace is not amnesty for the ledger.
It is bread acknowledged
while the debt remains legible.

The roof still leaks in one corner.
