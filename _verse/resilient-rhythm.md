Insecurity arrives like bad weather —
a voice that knows your passwords.

Resilience answers in rhythm,
not argument:
brass on the corner,
oak on St. Charles holding its ground,
second-line feet insisting
the body still belongs to itself.

Mardi Gras color does not erase the doubt.
It outvotes it for an afternoon.

Creole heat on the plate,
jazz in the chest —
not cure,
accompaniment.

The doubt returns on schedule.
So does the drum.

The drum answers first.
