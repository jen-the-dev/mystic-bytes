In New Orleans longing for echo
is ordinary weather —
name that carries past the block,
legacy not anecdote.

Acclaim seduces with smile.
Standing tall collects followers
and invoices you for their appetite.

Fame dances on thin flame —
joy and sorrow same mouth.
Wealth stain moralizes the glare.
Popularity weighs like night.

Allure never fades;
it just changes costume.

Tread with caution and grace.
Blessings and burdens share a door.

Dreams take wing only if you
keep footing when the crowd leaves.

The flame still dances. You still choose the tempo.
