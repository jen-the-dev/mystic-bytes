Parents' love is not a monument.
It is lunch,
correction,
the hug that arrives
after the harm you cannot undo.

Unwavering is what children need to believe.
Adults know it wavers —
sleep-deprived,
frightened,
fierce anyway.

Guidance is not innocence.
It is repetition
you spend years
trying to afford or forgive.

Nobody replaces that ledger.
Some entries bruise.
Some keep you vertical.

The lunchbox still closes with a click.
