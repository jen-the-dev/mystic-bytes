Creativity in New Orleans
is not a hobby.
It is how the city metabolizes trouble —
ordinary into loud,
loss into parade,
silence into horn.

Symphony is declined.
Pulse is accurate:
beat you feel in the bakery line,
idea that survives humidity,
mark that outlasts the landlord.

Talent without nerve rots here.
The night gets painted
because morning demands proof.

The pulse keeps time after the canvas dries.
