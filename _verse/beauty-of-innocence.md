Innocence is not ignorance marketed sweet.
It is cleanliness before the first bargain —
artless without being stupid,
naive without being property.

Modesty and restraint are not cages here.
They are early grammar
for a world that will later demand receipts.

Innocence speaks of goodness
as observed possibility,
not as completed civilization.

Selfishness has not yet learned the room.
Compassion still reads literal.
That is why the treasure feels gold-weighted:
you are witnessing a state
the adult world spends years unlearning.

Protect it where it is real.
Do not embalm it for display.

Cherish without turning it into brand.
Light shines when it is allowed to be temporary.

Eternity is the wrong pressure.
Innocence is beautiful because it ends.

The light still shines. You still notice before it changes.
