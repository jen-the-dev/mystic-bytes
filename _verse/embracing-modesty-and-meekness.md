Humility is not disappearance.
It is volume control on the self —
reserve where boast would be easier.

Modesty refuses the spotlight's rent.
Meekness is not fear.
It is choosing service
without auditioning for sainthood.

Contrition repairs what pride cracked —
not for applause,
for the integrity of the floor.

Gentleness is strength with the blade sheathed.
The loud room forgets quiet people
until the quiet people hold the door open
and everyone remembers who kept watch.

Wealth without humility is ballast in the wrong hold.
Rich spirit travels light.

Peace is not ignorance of cost.
It is knowing cost
and still offering the gentler hand.

The quiet still carries. You still choose low.
