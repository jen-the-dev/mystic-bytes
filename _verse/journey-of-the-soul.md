Pilgrimage is a polite word
for leaving one hunger
and discovering another.

The soul does not commute.
It accrues —
scars, appetites, small mercies,
the places you mispronounced
until they owned you.

Circumnavigation is vanity
when the crossing you need
is internal
and has no sponsors.

Who you become
is not the souvenir.
It is the toll.

The road keeps the count
whether you pose for it or not.
