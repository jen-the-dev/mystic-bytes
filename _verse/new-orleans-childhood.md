Children in New Orleans
learn rhythm before reason —
porch steps, parade routes,
the heat that makes running honest.

Not innocence as porcelain.
Innocence as sweat
and shared popsicles
and knowing which corners
belong to which cousins.

The playground is not safe.
It is familiar.
That is the gift
and the first debt.

Watch them climb.
The city is already writing them
into its brass ledger.

The popsicle melts before the song ends.
