The line is not philosophy.
It is calibration.

Pride stands tall — dignity, voice, land in the chest.
Arrogance is pride without listening,
pomp billed as grace.

Ego loves the boast.
Confidence without restraint
becomes weather that damages porches.

You want presence felt —
hourly, honestly —
not theater that collects enemies as souvenirs.

Noble pride wears dignity correctly:
lion that knows when to roar,
when to drink water,
when to leave the room.

Arrogance deceives with worth
borrowed from volume.

Find prideful mirth if you can —
brightness that does not require
someone smaller nearby.

The fence is thin. You still choose which side to stand on.
