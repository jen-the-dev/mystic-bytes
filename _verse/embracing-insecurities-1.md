Insecurity is not one feeling.
It is a committee —
doubt, hesitancy, the small heat
behind the ribs when the room sizes you.

Uncertainty removes the floor
one plank at a time
until footing feels like opinion.

Exposure terrifies because it is accurate:
you can be seen,
misread,
remembered wrong.

Warning bells ring on schedule.
They are not prophecy.
They are habit wearing a uniform.

Paralysis is the tax
on a mind that catalogs every exit
before entering the room.

You can face the fear
without becoming its employee.

Growth is not deleting the bells.
It is learning which alarm
belongs to the present
and which is old weather
stored in the body.

Self-assurance is built —
not declared —
from steps taken
while the bells still argue.

The bells still ring. You still cross the floor.
