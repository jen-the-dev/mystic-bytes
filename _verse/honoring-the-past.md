The past is not decoration.
It is load-bearing —
ancestors walking, loving, erring
in rooms we never saw.

Remembrance is not nostalgia tax.
It is how you trace the law
of your present shape:
which habits are inheritance,
which are revolt.

Memoir fades at the edges each year.
Lessons remain —
sometimes as gift,
sometimes as bruise you keep touching.

Honor is not freezing history in amber.
It is treasuring what still teaches
without hiring the dead as managers.

Mistakes belong to the archive too.
Growth is building forward
with materials you did not choose
and choices you finally own.

Future glow is not guaranteed.
It is built from what you kept,
what you returned,
what you refused to repeat.

The memory still holds. You still shape what follows.
