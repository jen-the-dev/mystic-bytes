Past is distant and load-bearing —
history held in memoir,
bygone days not decoration.

Ancestors walked, loved, erred
in rooms we never saw.
Memory passes hand to hand
like tool, not trophy.

Trace roots to read the law
of present shape:
inheritance, revolt, habit.

Lessons pervade as edges fade.
Mistakes in archive too.

Honor without embalming.
Treasure what still teaches.

Future glow is built, not promised.

The memory still holds. You still shape what follows.
