Imperfection is not an error in the blueprint.
It is how nature signs the work —
foible, scar, the asymmetry
that proves a hand was here.

Blemish mars the billboard.
It deepens the portrait.

Shortcoming is inventory everyone carries.
Character grows in the gap
between who you performed
and who you admitted.

Do not wish away the bars your body kept.
Some bars are mercy.
Some are story.

Connection arrives at the flaw line —
not despite it,
through it.

Quirks are not debris to hide.
They are coordinates.

Perfectly imperfect is not a slogan.
It is consent to be readable.

The scar still shows. You still call it yours.
