Parenthood is not halo.
It is lunch packed
while you are still hungry,
apology practiced
in front of a mirror
that will repeat your mistakes.

The spirit is not serenity.
It is revision —
the same lesson offered twice
without calling it revenge.

You inherit no manual.
You inherit watchfulness,
the terror of failing someone
who believes you are the weather.

Mercy is not forgetting the harm.
It is staying anyway
with your sleeves rolled.

The lunchbox closes. The lesson waits.
