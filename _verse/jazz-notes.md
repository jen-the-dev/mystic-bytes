Poetry in New Orleans
arrives second —
after the drum decides
the sentence length.

Congo Square still holds the receipt.
French Quarter sells the remix.
Gumbo, second line, Mississippi mud:
not props.
Evidence.

A stanza here is a bet
that language can swing
without stealing the solo.

Let the syllables sway.
Let them lose sometimes.

The drum still owns the downbeat.
