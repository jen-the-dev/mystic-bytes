Under a New Orleans moon,
forever is pronounced
before the jazz stops
and the practical world returns.

Cobblestones remember footsteps.
Magnolia remembers heat.
Kisses remember appetite —
not eternity.

French Quarter dark
is good for making promises
the morning will audit.

Eternal is a costume word.
What lasts longer
is the habit of returning
after the fight,
or the habit of not.

The music stops. The promise stays unpaid.
