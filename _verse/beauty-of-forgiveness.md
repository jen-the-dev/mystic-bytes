Beauty here is not soft light.
It is the seam where harm was sewn
and someone chose not to pull harder.

Forgiveness does not beautify the wound.
It refuses to make the wound
your only room.

The lovely part — if lovely applies —
is the hand unclenching
while memory keeps its teeth.

You may admire the seam
without calling it healed.

The teeth still remember.
