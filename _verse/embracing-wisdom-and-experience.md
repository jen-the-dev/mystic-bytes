Maturity is not a birthday.
It is accumulation —
error translated into reflex,
appetite taught to wait.

Sophistication is style after consequence.
You learn which entrances cost too much.

Wisdom is not certainty.
It is better questions
after the old answers bruised you.

Experience is the foundation
no one photographs:
nights spent correcting,
mornings spent continuing.

Ripe means ready for the climb
you once postponed
for a prettier story about yourself.

Never compromise is a child's banner.
Adults compromise and keep receipts.

Seek learning in speech and silence.
Happiness here is not constant sun.
It is alignment —
body, nerve, and the work you claim.

The climb continues. You still carry what you learned.
