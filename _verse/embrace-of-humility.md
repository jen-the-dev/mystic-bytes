In New Orleans humility keeps odd hours —
overlooked virtue, loud city,
quiet power in the aftermath.

Modesty walks the maze without audition.
No limelight hunger,
no praise invoice —
helping hand extended
because the bridge needed hands.

Contrition mends what pride cracked:
bridges, names, the small wars
ego starts and cannot finish.

Wealth here is unburdened spirit —
joy without billboard,
richness that does not perform.

Each humble act lights a block
the parade forgot to illuminate.

Tapestry is declined.
Thread is accurate —
one act, one glow, one day.

The glow still spreads. You still walk unburdened.
