Anxiety in New Orleans
learns to dance or leave.
Mardi Gras rhythm files it elsewhere.
Jazz in the French Quarter
offers a slower pulse
the nervous system can borrow.

Peace is not silence here.
It is syncopation —
worry losing the downbeat
to somebody else's solo.

Café chicory is medicine.
Poets tell the truth with cream in it.
Garden District shade
does not erase the bill.
It postpones collection.

The pulse still soothes after the cup empties.
