The divine is not a resort.
It is the crossing you keep reaching
after the brochure lied about distance.

Journey is the honest word —
blisters, backtracking,
the dog at the ditch
that will not bless your pace.

Hecate waits without applauding.
She is not the destination.
She is the witness
that you did not turn back
for the easier story.

Arrival is a rumor.
The mile in front of you is real.

The blister speaks before you do.
