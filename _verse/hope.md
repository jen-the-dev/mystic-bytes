Hope is not a guarantee.
It is the decision to keep a calendar
after the year has already failed you once.

Aspiration names a direction.
It does not promise arrival.
The body learns the difference
when optimism runs out of stationery.

Faith in a brighter future
is useful only if it admits
the dark room you are standing in.

Hope that refuses inventory
is propaganda wearing soft light.

The calendar stays open to tomorrow.
