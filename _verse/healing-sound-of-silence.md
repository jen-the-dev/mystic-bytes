Noise was the costume.
Silence is the fitting room.

Under the hum of screens and small talk,
something in the body
has been trying to finish a sentence
for years.

When the sound drops,
you hear it —
not peace,
not yet —
the accurate size of your own grief.

Healing is not quiet arriving
like a gift bag.
It is quiet endured
long enough to stop flinching
at your own name.

The ringing continues a while after.
