Solitude in New Orleans
is loud before it isn't.
You think loneliness owns the block
until jazz from the French Quarter
reaches your kitchen.

Symphony is declined.
Communal melody is accurate —
gas lamp friendship,
bayou breath,
cemetery echo
reminding you
the dead also traveled in company.

Alone is not sentenced here.
It is intermission.

The brass finds you on the next corner.
