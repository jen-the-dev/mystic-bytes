Devotion is not fever.
It is showing up
after the fever has filed its report.

The altar of daily work —
dishes, patience, the same route
to the same grief —
is less photogenic than surrender
and more difficult to fake.

You do not owe the universe
your performance of loyalty.
You owe the practice
your honest attendance.

Devotion ends where martyrdom begins.
The line is thinner than scripture admits.

The practice keeps its own hours.
