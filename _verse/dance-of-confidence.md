In New Orleans confidence learns to dance —
not stomp, not pose for the passing tour,
but move with the band's inconvenient tempo.

Esteem is posture with memory:
storms survived, debts negotiated,
a laugh that does not beg permission.

Mastering it is craft, not armor polish.
Sharp confidence cuts the room;
humble confidence feeds it.

The city teaches the duet —
tenacity on one foot,
restraint on the other.

Dignity is not silence.
It is choosing your volume
before the challenge names your price.

Belief is not noise.
It is the inner vote
that keeps walking when doubt
offers the easier chair.

Swagger is borrowed weather.
Skill is what remains
when the horn section leaves.

The dance continues. You still find the beat.
