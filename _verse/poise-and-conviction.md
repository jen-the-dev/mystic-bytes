Confidence is desired
because the room keeps score.

Poise is posture with memory.
Morale wavers. You adjust.

Brashness is confidence without calibration —
cuts the room,
collects the bill later.

Balance is the art:
tenacity on one foot,
restraint on the other.

Dignity is not silence.
It is choosing your volume
before the race names your price.

Belief is not noise.
It is the inner vote
that keeps walking when doubt
offers the easier chair.

Trust the inner voice
enough to test it in public.
Rejoice is too strong a word.
Accuracy is enough.

Cultivate inside first.
The life that comes alive
is the one that can fail in daylight
and continue.

The conviction still holds. You still navigate.
