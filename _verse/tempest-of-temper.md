Anger arrives as pressure change —
jaw locked, vision narrowed,
the room suddenly too small
for the argument it contains.

The narrative does not romanticize the storm.
It records the damage:
judgment clouded,
relationships scorched at the edges,
a residue of bitterness
that lasts longer than the flash.

Temper is not power.
It is combustion
mistaken for clarity.

When the weather clears,
the furniture still smells of smoke.

The jaw unlocks one notch. The smoke remains.
