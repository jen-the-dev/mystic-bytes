Passion wakes in New Orleans
like weather you did not schedule —
heat in the chest,
brass in the blood,
the room suddenly too small
for the number of wants.

Hearth and wildfire share a wick.
One cooks.
One evacuates.

Respect is knowing which flame
you are feeding
when the body votes yes.

Caution is not celibacy.
It is keeping the match
away from the curtain
while still admitting you like fire.

The curtain still remembers smoke.
