Virtue is not a halo you inherit.
It is labor —
the harder right
when the shortcut winks from the corner bar.

Righteousness starts indoors,
in the small rooms no committee audits:
what you return, what you refuse, what you name out loud.

Kindness is not softness for its own sake.
It is muscle —
lifting someone else's weather
without requiring a receipt.

Honor ages better than charm.
Charm gets you seated;
honor gets you home
with your name still intact.

No one becomes a paragon by announcement.
You become it the way brass learns a tune —
repeat, miss, repeat,
until the line lands true
even when the crowd stops listening.

The code still holds when the light goes dim.
